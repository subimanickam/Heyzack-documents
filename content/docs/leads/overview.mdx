The Leads section is where you track every prospective customer from first contact through to completed installation. Each lead represents a single sales opportunity tied to a customer, and the portal gives you a structured way to capture contact details, assign priorities, log notes, attach documents, and monitor progress through each stage of your pipeline.

## What a lead contains

Each lead record stores the following information:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| **Lead name** | A unique label for the opportunity (e.g. "Smith Residence – Solar Install") |
| **Customer name** | The name of the end customer |
| **Contact number** | Customer phone number |
| **Email** | Customer email address |
| **Location** | Site or service address |
| **Status** | Current stage in the sales pipeline |
| **Priority** | Urgency level: High, Medium, or Low |
| **Source** | How the lead originated (pulled from a dynamic list) |
| **Value** | Estimated deal value in EUR |
| **Assigned to** | The team member responsible for this lead |
| **Notes** | Free-text notes added over time |
| **Quotes** | Quotes attached to the lead |

<Note>
Priority labels support both English and French. In the portal, you will see the English label (High, Medium, or Low) in priority badges regardless of the interface language setting.
</Note>

## Two ways to view your leads

The portal gives you two view modes for browsing your pipeline. Toggle between them using the **List** and **Kanban** buttons in the filter bar.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="List view">
    List view displays all leads in a paginated table, five leads per page. Each row shows the lead name, customer name, status badge, priority badge, contact number, location, creation date, source, assigned team member, and deal value at a glance.

    Use list view when you need to scan a large number of leads, compare values, or quickly jump to a specific record.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Kanban view">
    Kanban view organises leads into vertical columns, one column per status. You can drag a lead card from one column and drop it into another to update its status without opening the lead.

    Use Kanban view when you want a visual snapshot of how your pipeline is distributed across stages.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Searching and filtering

A search bar and a status filter appear above the lead list on every view. You can use them together or independently.

- **Search** matches against customer name, contact number, and location.
- **Status filter** narrows the list to a single pipeline stage, or shows all leads when set to "All".

The lead count in the list header updates automatically to reflect how many leads match your current filters.
